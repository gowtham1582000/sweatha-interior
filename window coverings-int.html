Venetian Blinds

A Venetian blind is a style of window covering comprised of horizontal slats that overlap and are normally drawn together and lowered by pulling a lead. Often, hard materials like aluminium, plastic, or wood are used to create the slats, which are coordinated by a network of wires running through the blind.

Vertical Blinds

The vertical fabric louvres or slats that make up vertical blinds can be twisted to close and block the window or extended widely to let in a lot of light. The cord that controls the louvres is attached to a chain that runs along the far right or left of the blind.

Roller Blinds

A roller blind is a single piece of cloth that slips into the top of your window frame, either inside or outside of your window break, and wraps around a casing.

Zebra Blinds

Modern roller blind alternatives to horizontal blinds include zebra blinds, often known as dual blinds. These blinds effortlessly transition from day to night because they include horizontal, opaque and translucent fabric stripes that alternate.


Roman Blinds

Roman shades are, at their most basic, just fabric window treatments that may be raised or lowered with the aid of a lead system and rings sewed on the fabric's reverse. The fabric stacks uniformly in horizontal folds when elevated.

Wooden Blinds

A conventional window blind consists of numerous long, horizontal or vertical slats made of different kinds of hard material, such as wood, plastic, or metal, that are connected by cords that pass between the slats.

PVC Blinds

A standard window PVC blind is formed of numerous long, horizontal or vertical slats of wood, plastic, metal, or other hard material that are connected by cables that pass between the slats.
